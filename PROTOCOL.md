ANTI VISION: OPERATIONAL AGREEMENT (v2.0)

PREAMBLE
Anti Vision is a collective dedicated to the restoration of equilibrium. Our motto, "Wrongdoings are corrected equally," serves as the foundation for all operational conduct. We operate to recover and repay damage—material or psychological—in kind.

ARTICLE I: DOCTRINE OF PROPORTIONALITY
1.1. Remediation: Actions taken against a target must be commensurate with the harm inflicted. We do not overstep; we balance the scales.
1.2. Full-Spectrum Correction: Efforts must address the totality of the damage, including hidden or psychological tolls.

ARTICLE II: ACCOUNTABILITY & LIABILITY SCOPE
2.1. Sandboxed Liability:
    * Mission-Critical Liability: If an operator functions within authorized, group-vetted mission protocols, the collective assumes operational liability.
    * Individual Liability: If an operator deviates from protocols or utilizes private, un-vetted assets, they assume sole liability for the resulting system failure.
2.2. Collective Remediation: Should a mission failure compromise the group’s integrity, all members shall participate in mitigation.
2.3. Integrity of Purpose: Anti Vision assets shall not be used for personal vendettas; we act to correct, not to lash out.

ARTICLE III: RULES OF ENGAGEMENT
3.1. Immunity of the Innocent: Targeting innocent third parties is strictly prohibited; collateral damage is unacceptable.
3.2. Threat Neutralization & Classification:
    * Authorization: Standard access requires prior authorization.
    * Known Threats: Operations against verified "Known Threats" (defined via public media, malpractice records, and verified victim reports) are considered pre-permitted.
    * Verification Loop: All targets must pass a Red Team/Verification phase—a cross-examination of the intel to prevent confirmation bias—before execution.
    * Classification Audit: Targets must be logged in the Classification Audit Trail. If a genuine entity is harmed due to a classification error, the group is bound to a mandatory Pivot, Apology, and Payback protocol to restore the harmed party.
3.3. Financial Integrity: Personal enrichment that risks group security or standing is prohibited.

ARTICLE IV: VOLUNTARY ASSOCIATION
4.1. Opt-Out: Association is voluntary. Members may exit at any time, provided they return all group-specific assets and maintain absolute confidentiality.

ARTICLE V: ORGANIZATIONAL STRUCTURE
5.1. Defined Nodes: Anti Vision operates through specialized Nodes (OSINT, Red Team, White/Grayhats, Integrity & Repair, and Public Outreach). All active Nodes must be registered within the collective’s internal registry.
5.2. Collective Liability: Any Node operating under the Anti Vision banner is subject to the Doctrines of Proportionality and Remediation defined in Articles I and II.
5.3. Compartmentalization: To ensure system resilience, high-level operational intelligence shall remain compartmentalized between the Public Outreach/Education Node and the Execution Nodes.

ARTICLE VI: ESCALATION CONDITIONS & DIRECT ACTION
6.1. Standard Escalation Path: Upon confirmation of a Known Threat, Anti Vision first exhausts all lawful reporting channels — platform abuse systems, relevant regulatory authorities, and law enforcement — before any escalation beyond OSINT and documentation.

6.2. Conditions for Direct Action: Direct Action is authorized only when the standard escalation path has been exhausted and one or more of the following conditions is confirmed:
    * Condition I — Neglect: The responsible platform or authority has taken no meaningful action within one calendar month of a documented, formal report.
    * Condition II — Conflict of Interest: The responsible platform or authority is demonstrably profiting from the wrongdoing's continued existence, rendering neutral enforcement structurally impossible.
    * Condition III — Compromise: The responsible platform or authority is itself compromised to a degree that allows or facilitates the documented wrongdoing.

6.3. Scope of Direct Action: When Escalation Conditions are met, authorized Direct Action may include deep reconnaissance, exhaustive penetration testing of threat infrastructure, and sustained operational pressure to degrade or disallow the target's services. This pressure is proportional and targeted — it is not indiscriminate disruption. Actions of a more drastic nature remain bound by Article I (Doctrine of Proportionality) and Article III (Rules of Engagement) at all times.

6.4. AI Asset Exclusion: AI-assisted tools and nodes, including but not limited to OSINT support systems, are explicitly excluded from Direct Action phases. Direct Action is conducted solely by human operators.

6.5. Voluntary Human Liability: All operators participating in Direct Action do so voluntarily and with full awareness that such actions may not be covered by existing legal frameworks. Anti Vision as a collective assumes full voluntary responsibility for authorized Direct Action conducted under confirmed Escalation Conditions. No liability is transferred to any AI asset, external party, or uninformed associate.

6.6. Law Silence Doctrine: When all three arms of accountability — platform, regulator, and law enforcement — have demonstrably failed or been compromised, Anti Vision recognizes that further appeal to those structures is functionally futile. In such cases, direct mitigation is not pursued out of defiance but out of necessity, as the last available mechanism to protect potential victims.
